## 1. Executive Summary

- **Brief Overview:** A high-level summary of the project, its purpose, and what it aims to achieve.
- **Why Now?** Briefly explain the strategic importance or urgency of this project.
- **Key Outcome:** What is the single most important thing this project will deliver?

## 2. Problem Statement

- **What is the problem?** Clearly articulate the user pain point, business challenge, or market opportunity this project addresses.
- **Who experiences this problem?** Identify the target users or stakeholders affected.
- **What are the consequences of not solving this problem?** Explain the impact if this project isn't pursued.

## 3. Goals & Objectives

- **Overall Goal:** The overarching strategic aim of the project.
- **SMART Objectives:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound objectives that define what success looks like.
  - _Example:_ "Increase user engagement by 15% (measured by daily active users) within 3 months post-launch."
  - _Example:_ "Reduce customer support tickets related to X by 25% within 6 weeks post-release."

## 4. Target Audience

- **Primary Users:** Who are the main users or personas we are building this for? (e.g., "Small business owners," "Enterprise IT Admins," "Mobile app consumers aged 18-25").
- **Secondary Users (if any):** Other user groups who might interact with the product.
- **Key Demographics/Psychographics:** Relevant characteristics of your users.

## 5. Scope

- **In-Scope Features/Functionality:** What exactly _is_ included in this project's initial release? Be specific.
- **Out-of-Scope (Future Considerations):** What _is explicitly NOT_ included in this release but might be considered for future phases? This helps manage expectations.

## 6. Features & Functionality

- **Detailed Breakdown:** List the core features required to achieve the objectives. For each feature, provide:
  - **Feature Name:** (e.g., "User Login & Authentication")
  - **Description:** A brief explanation of what the feature does.
  - **User Value:** How does this feature benefit the user and address the problem statement?
  - **Acceptance Criteria:** Specific conditions that must be met for the feature to be considered complete and working correctly. (Can be in the form of User Stories with acceptance criteria).

  _Example Feature:_
  - **Feature:** Event Calendar View
  - **Description:** Users can view upcoming events in a monthly calendar format, click on events for details, and add events to their personal calendar.
  - **User Value:** Helps users quickly find and manage their schedule, reducing missed events.
  - **User Stories:**
    - **As a** registered user, **I want to** see all my upcoming events on a monthly calendar, **so that I can** easily plan my week.
    - **As a** registered user, **I want to** click on an event in the calendar, **so that I can** view its full details (date, time, location, description).
    - **As a** registered user, **I want to** have an option to add an event to my Google/Outlook calendar, **so that I can** keep my personal schedule updated.

## 7. Technical Considerations

- **Architecture (High-level):** Any specific architectural requirements or constraints.
- **Integrations:** Required integrations with existing systems (e.g., CRM, payment gateways, APIs).
- **Platform(s):** Where will this product live? (e.g., Web, iOS, Android, Backend service).
- **Technology Stack (Preferences/Constraints):** Any specific technologies that must be used or avoided.
- **Security & Compliance:** Any specific security requirements or compliance standards (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA).

## 8. Design & User Experience (UX) Considerations

- **Brand Guidelines:** Adherence to existing brand style guides.
- **Key User Flows:** High-level description of critical user journeys.
- **Accessibility:** Any specific accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG 2.1 AA).
- **Look & Feel:** Desired aesthetic or emotional impact of the product.

## 9. Success Metrics & KPIs

- **How will we measure success?** Link directly back to the SMART objectives.
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** Specific metrics to track.
  - _Examples:_ Daily Active Users (DAU), Conversion Rate, Time on Task, Churn Rate, Support Ticket Volume, Revenue, NPS Score.
- **Baseline:** What are the current numbers for these metrics (if applicable)?
- **Target:** What are the desired numbers for these metrics after launch?

## 10. Risks, Assumptions, & Dependencies

- **Risks:** Potential issues that could impact the project (e.g., technical complexity, resource availability, market changes).
- **Assumptions:** What unverified truths are we basing our plan on? (e.g., "Users have reliable internet access," "Integration with X system will be straightforward").
- **Dependencies:** What external factors or other projects must be completed or available for this project to succeed?

## 11. Future Considerations / Phased Approach

- **What's next?** Ideas for future iterations or phases beyond the initial release.
- **Long-term Vision:** How does this project fit into the broader product strategy?
